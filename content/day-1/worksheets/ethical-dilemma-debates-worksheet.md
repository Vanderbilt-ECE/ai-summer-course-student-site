# Ethical Dilemma Debates Worksheet

## Scenario A — Hiring Algorithm

A company uses an AI to screen résumés. The AI was trained on 10 years of successful employee data. It consistently rates women and candidates from HBCUs lower — not because of explicit bias, but because historical hiring skewed toward white men from Ivy League schools. The AI is accurate at predicting who *was* hired, not who *should be* hired. Should the company use it?

## Scenario B — AI in the Classroom

A professor assigns an essay. A student uses Claude to generate a first draft, then heavily edits it — changing the argument, adding their own research, and rewriting most sentences. The final essay is 80% original work. The student didn't disclose they used AI. Did they cheat? Should there be a rule? What should the rule be?

## Scenario C — Facial Recognition in Public

A city installs AI-powered cameras in its downtown area. The system can identify individuals with 95% accuracy and has already helped solve 12 crimes. It also incorrectly flags people of color at twice the rate of white individuals. The city says the overall crime reduction justifies the system. A civil liberties group disagrees. Who's right?

## Scenario D — AI Health Diagnosis

An AI system diagnoses a rare disease with 98% accuracy — better than most doctors. A patient's doctor disagrees with the AI's diagnosis and wants to run further (expensive, time-consuming) tests before treating. The hospital's insurance provider says the AI's diagnosis is sufficient and won't cover further testing. Should the doctor defer to the AI?

## Scenario E — The Cloned Voice

A company creates an AI tool that can clone anyone's voice from 30 seconds of audio. It's marketed for audiobook narration and accessibility (giving voice back to people who've lost theirs). The same tool is used to generate a fake audio clip of a politician saying something they never said, and it goes viral before it's debunked. Should the tool have been released? Who's responsible for the fake clip — the company, the person who made it, or the platforms that spread it?
